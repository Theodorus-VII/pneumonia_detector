About Us

What is this project?

This AI-Powered Pneumonia Diagnosis tool is a web-based application that uses artificial intelligence to assist in the diagnosis of pneumonia from chest radiography (X-rays). Pneumonia is a serious lung infection that can affect anyone, especially children, elderly, and people with weak immune systems. It can cause fever, cough, difficulty breathing, and even death. According to the World Health Organization, pneumonia is the leading cause of death among children under five years old, killing more than 800,000 children every year. Therefore, early and accurate diagnosis of pneumonia is crucial for effective treatment and prevention.

We are three students who created this project as part of a University Project. We wanted to learn more about machine learning and deep learning, and apply them to a real-world problem.

How did we make it?

We used Python and TensorFlow to train our model on an open source dataset of 5863 chest X-ray images, labeled into two categories: pneumonia and normal. We used a convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture to build our pneumonia detection system. As part of the integration into a web-app, we also used TensorFlow.js to run our model in the browser, ensuring privacy and accessibility for our users. No data is sent to a server, and the model and prediction are run locally directly in the browser. The code for our project is also open source and is available on GitHub.

Pneumonia is a common and potentially serious lung infection that can affect people of all ages. It is usually diagnosed by a doctor based on the patient's symptoms, medical history, physical examination, and chest X-ray. However, diagnosing pneumonia can be challenging, especially in low-resource settings where access to medical facilities and equipment is limited. Moreover, chest X-ray images can be difficult to interpret, and different radiologists may sometimes have different opinions on the final diagnosis.

That is why we think that an AI system can be a useful tool for assisting doctors in diagnosing pneumonia. Our system can analyze chest X-ray images and give a probability score for pneumonia. This can help doctors add another layer of security to their diagnosis, and avoid false negatives.

Why did we make it?

However, our system is not a replacement for normal medical diagnosis, and it should not be used as the sole basis for any health decisions. We always recommend that users consult a doctor for professional advice and treatment.

We hope that our project can help people who are at risk of pneumonia, especially in developing countries where access to medical facilities and resources is limited. We also hope that our project can inspire other students and developers to use AI for social good.
